1. The sample JD I'm critiquing

A fictional Marketing Manager role at a 30-person B2B SaaS company. This is the kind of draft that looks finished but has the exact issues you flagged in your own doc: vague duties, redundant points, soft KPI alignment.

Marketing Manager — Acme Cloud Platform
Full-time · Remote (US) · $90,000–$120,000 + equity · Reports to: VP of Marketing
About the role

Acme Cloud is looking for a results-driven Marketing Manager to join our growing team. This is an exciting opportunity for the right candidate to make a real impact in a fast-paced environment. We're a passionate team building the future of cloud infrastructure.

2. Annotated critique — line by line

This is the "track changes" view. Original text on top (red strikethrough = needs cutting/fixing), proposed rewrite below (green = recommended language), with a one-line rationale. In the actual deliverable I send this as a Word .docx with native Track Changes plus inline comments — same content, same rationale, native review tools.

Duties & responsibilities — the main concern

1
Original: Drive marketing initiatives across the funnel
Suggested: Own demand-gen for the mid-market segment, from first-touch through MQL handoff to sales (target: $X ARR pipeline / quarter).

"Drive" + "initiatives" + "across the funnel" is three vague abstractions stacked. Without a segment, a handoff point, or a number, this is unfalsifiable — meaning the candidate can't tell whether they're succeeding.

2
Original: Develop and execute marketing campaigns
Suggested: Plan and ship 2–3 integrated campaigns per quarter (e.g., webinar + email sequence + paid promotion + sales enablement), measured against the pipeline target above.

This was redundant with #1. Specify the cadence and what an integrated campaign actually contains — otherwise candidates will assume "one email blast" satisfies it.

3
Original: Manage marketing programs and projects
Suggested: Delete this line.

Fully redundant with #1 and #2. "Programs and projects" is the umbrella term that ALL the other duties live under — listing it adds no information and signals to the candidate that the JD wasn't tightened.

4
Original: Work cross-functionally with sales, product, and engineering teams
Suggested: Partner with Sales (weekly MQL pipeline review with AEs) and Product Marketing (monthly persona/messaging refresh based on win/loss data).

"Work cross-functionally" appears in 90% of B2B JDs and tells the candidate nothing. Specify which teams, what the working ritual is, and what the output looks like.

5
Original: Analyze marketing performance and optimize campaigns
Suggested: Own the weekly marketing dashboard (Hubspot + Looker). Report MQL→SQL conversion, CPL by channel, and campaign attribution every Monday to the leadership team.

"Analyze and optimize" doesn't say what tooling, what cadence, or who the report goes to. Specifying the dashboard and the audience makes the candidate self-select for someone comfortable reporting up.

6
Original: Stay current on industry trends and best practices
Suggested: Delete.

This is filler. No serious candidate doesn't do this — it's table stakes for the role, not a duty. Listing it dilutes the duties that actually matter.

7
Original: Collaborate with internal stakeholders to align marketing activities with business goals
Suggested: Delete — covered by the rewritten #4.

"Collaborate with stakeholders" is a more abstract version of "work cross-functionally" (#4). Cutting it brings the duties list from 8 items down to 5, all concrete.

8
Original: Contribute to overall marketing strategy and planning
Suggested: Co-author the annual marketing plan with the VP and own the quarterly OKR for mid-market demand-gen.

"Contribute to strategy" reads as filler unless it specifies what they own. Adding the quarterly OKR makes the seniority and accountability legible.

About the role & "what we offer" — quick fixes

9
Original: Acme Cloud is looking for a results-driven Marketing Manager to join our growing team. This is an exciting opportunity for the right candidate to make a real impact in a fast-paced environment.
Suggested: We're hiring a Marketing Manager to own demand-gen for our mid-market segment. You'll join a 6-person marketing team reporting to the VP, with a $400K/yr program budget and full autonomy over channel mix.

"Results-driven," "exciting opportunity," "real impact," "fast-paced" — every phrase here is a cliché the candidate scans past. Substitute facts: team size, budget, autonomy, who they report to. This is where you lose 40% of qualified candidates if it reads generic.

10
Original: Great team (in "What we offer")
Suggested: Delete or replace with something concrete (e.g., "Quarterly in-person off-sites in Tahoe; documented async-first culture; founder-led with 18 months of runway")

Every JD says "great team." Cut it or earn it with specifics.

3. Summary report — the four focal areas

This is the 1–2 page report you'd get in the deliverable, summarizing where the JD is strong, where it's weak, and the prioritized fixes. Same four focal areas you requested.

Job role clarity
⚠ Needs work — biggest gap

Duties read as 8 separate items but are really 4–5 ideas restated. After the proposed cuts (3 deletions, 5 rewrites), the candidate gets a crisp picture of the role's scope in 30 seconds of reading.

  • Cut: lines 3, 6, 7 (full redundancy)
  • Rewrite: lines 1, 2, 4, 5, 8 (vague → concrete)
  • Add segment ownership (mid-market) and the quarterly OKR — currently absent
Growth opportunities
✗ Missing — entirely silent

The JD says nothing about what success at 12/24 months looks like, or what role this leads into. Strong candidates filter heavily on growth path — its absence is the second-biggest weakness after duties clarity.

  • Add a "Growth path" section: 12-month target = full ownership of mid-market segment; 24-month = Sr. Manager or Director if hitting OKRs
  • Mention specific past hires who grew (anonymized if needed): "Our last MM hire promoted to Sr. in 18 months"
Work environment
⚠ Generic — needs specifics

"Remote-friendly culture" + "flexible PTO" + "great team" reads as table stakes. Strong candidates ignore generic descriptions and look for friction signals.

  • Specify: meeting cadence (X hours/week meetings), async tooling (Linear/Slack/Loom), in-person frequency, time-zone overlap requirement
  • Be honest about pace: "intense for ~3 weeks per quarter at campaign launches; slower otherwise" — beats "fast-paced"
KPI alignment & relevance
⚠ Half the KPIs don't match the role

See the KPI section below for the full breakdown. Headline: 3 of 6 listed KPIs aren't actually under this role's control. Replace with metrics that are.

4. KPI alignment review

Per-KPI verdict on whether the metric (a) reflects this role's actual control, (b) ladders up to revenue, and (c) is measurable without ambiguity.

KPI Verdict Why
Marketing-qualified leads (MQLs) generated Keep Direct, controllable, ladders to pipeline. Add a numeric target (e.g., 250 MQLs / quarter) so the KPI is binary-checkable.
Pipeline contribution Keep This is the right north-star KPI for a Marketing Manager. Specify attribution model (first-touch / multi-touch / sourced vs. influenced) — without that, the metric is contested.
Brand awareness Drop Not measurable at this seniority. "Brand awareness" sits at the CMO/VP level with budget for brand tracking studies. A Marketing Manager can influence but not own it.
Campaign ROI Rewrite Too generic. Specify which campaigns and what ROI means here: "blended channel CAC for paid programs < $X" is much clearer than "Campaign ROI."
Website traffic growth Drop Traffic is a vanity metric for a B2B MM role. Replace with conversion metrics: "Mid-market organic traffic → free-trial conversion rate."
Social media engagement Drop Not owned by this role (sits with Content or Brand). Including it dilutes signal and gives candidates a metric they can't move.
— Add MQL → SQL conversion rate: lagging indicator of lead quality. Most MMs are judged purely on MQL volume; adding conversion forces a quality focus.
— Add Campaign cycle time: weeks from kickoff to launch. Reveals operational maturity — a more diagnostic KPI than ROI alone.
Net change

6 KPIs → 5 KPIs after the review (2 kept as-is with targets added, 1 rewritten, 3 dropped, 2 added). The new set is fully under the role's control and ladders cleanly to ARR.

5. How I'd approach your document

Exactly the same methodology — applied to whatever role and KPI sheet you send over.

  1. Read pass (no edits). First read is for the role itself — who you're hiring, what they own, what success looks like. I take notes on what's not said as much as what is.
  2. Duties audit. Each duty gets one of four verdicts: keep, rewrite (with proposed language), delete-as-redundant, or split-into-two. Track Changes in Word with comments explaining the call.
  3. KPI alignment table. Per-KPI verdict using the controllability × measurability × revenue-ladder grid (see section 4 above). I add 1–3 KPIs that are missing and ladder better.
  4. Four focal-area scoring. Each of your focal areas gets a one-paragraph verdict + bullet list of prioritized fixes. Scored: ✓ strong / ⚠ needs work / ✗ missing.
  5. Prioritized fix list. The summary report opens with the top 3 highest-leverage changes — usually 1 in duties, 1 in growth path, 1 in KPIs. If you only do 3 things from the critique, do these.
  6. Delivery. You get: (a) your original .docx with native Track Changes + comments, (b) a separate 1.5-page summary report .pdf or .docx (your preference). Turnaround: 48 hours from receiving your doc.
